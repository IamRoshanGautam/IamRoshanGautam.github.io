My Story

Hello. I'm Rosh.

I'm originally from Kathmandu, Nepal. Growing up there taught me something valuable: when you're surrounded by mountains and navigating a city that's equal parts chaos and charm, you learn to see patterns in complexity. You learn that systems, whether they're traffic flows or software pipelines, need someone who can step back and ask the right questions.

That mindset followed me into tech. At Verisk, I worked as a QA Automation Engineer across the full testing spectrum: UI testing, API validation, and performance testing for data-intensive platforms. But what really shaped me was being embedded in a product team. I wasn't just testing in isolation. I was in the room with developers, product owners, and business stakeholders from requirement refinement all the way through deployment. I saw how features were born from business needs, how trade-offs were made, and how technical decisions impacted what users actually experienced.

That experience changed everything. I realized I wasn't just interested in whether the UI rendered correctly or the API performed under load. I wanted to understand why we were building it, who it served, and what business problem we were actually solving. That's when quality assurance became a lens for product thinking.

So I pursued my MS in Management Information Systems at Southern Illinois University Edwardsville to formalize what I'd been learning in practice: how to bridge technology and business strategy. Stepping out of my comfort zone and into the American Midwest has been its own education. The cultural differences, the new ways of thinking, the different perspectives on work and life, all of it has sharpened my adaptability and deepened how I approach problems. When you learn to navigate unfamiliar territory, you get better at seeing solutions others might miss.

My capstone project right now applies exactly that, using data analytics and systems thinking to solve a real business challenge.

Here's what I bring: deep technical experience across UI testing, API validation, performance optimization, and test automation, combined with real product team exposure. I know how to work cross-functionally, translate technical constraints into business language, and think from the user backward. I've sat in refinement sessions, negotiated acceptance criteria, and shipped features that mattered.

I'm actively seeking opportunities where this combination creates real value. Currently based in Southern Illinois and open to relocation, I'm excited about roles in Product Management, Quality Assurance Engineering, Business Analysis, Technical Business Analysis, Data Analytics, or any techno-functional position where strategic thinking meets hands-on execution. I'm open to exploring diverse paths where I can leverage my unique blend of technical depth and business acumen to drive meaningful impact.

If you're building something worth building, let's talk.

SIUE Nexus: Maximizing Career Success Through Digital Alumni Integration

Authors: Roshan Gautam, Kayleigh Straeter, Joseph Meyer, Erica Dockery, Prizma Bajracharya

Course: CMIS 526 - Information System and Technology

Abstract: SIUE Nexus is a strategic digital initiative designed to transform career outcomes at Southern Illinois University Edwardsville by connecting students with a powerful alumni network. The proposed platform addresses the current disconnect through AI-driven matchmaking, structured mentorships, event management, and engagement analytics.

Objectives:

  • Enhance student career success through alumni mentorship
  • Enable structured networking across geographical boundaries
  • Strengthen alumni loyalty and university reputation

Platform Features:

  • Customizable profile system for students and alumni
  • AI-driven mentor-mentee matching based on career trajectory
  • Event module for hybrid networking sessions with calendar sync
  • Feedback and engagement tracking for continual improvement
  • Data dashboards (Power BI, Argos) for trend analysis and ROI measurement

Technology Ecosystem: Integrated with Ellucian Banner, Microsoft Power BI, Argos, Microsoft PowerApps, and secure cloud infrastructure

Recommendation: Graduway by Gravyty was recommended based on feature completeness, alignment with stakeholder needs, and measurable ROI. Alternatives (Scale Growth AI and 360 Alumni) were also evaluated for budget and scope tradeoffs.

Benefits: $680,000 projected in five-year gains from improved engagement, event participation, and alumni giving. ROI: 30.4%. Payback Period: 1.44 years.

Conclusion: SIUE Nexus is not just a platform — it's a cultural shift toward long-term alumni-student synergy. With proper implementation, it will strengthen SIUE's identity, elevate its placement success, and build a thriving professional community.

Acoustic Side-Channel Risk via High-DPI Mice

Course: CMIS 422 – Principles of Information Security

Type: Graduate Paper / Emerging Threat & Risk Analysis

Executive Summary: This paper examines an emerging acoustic side-channel risk where high-performance optical mice can capture micro-vibrations on a desk surface. Under certain conditions, these signals can be processed to infer portions of nearby speech — showing why "microphone off" may not fully protect sensitive discussions.

Mitigations proposed:

  • Peripheral governance (approved devices and configuration baselines)
  • Endpoint hardening (limit access to raw HID streams and strengthen application controls)
  • Operational safeguards for high-sensitivity meetings and conversations

Main reference: Fakih et al. (2025), "Invisible ears at your fingertips" (arXiv).
